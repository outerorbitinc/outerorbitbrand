02 / Spacing

4-point base, multiplied.

A strict 4px scale. Never use arbitrary values in product code; if you need something off-scale, add it here first. Most UI uses sp-2, sp-4, sp-6.

03 / Radii & Elevation

Mostly sharp. Sparingly soft.

Hardware-grade radii — 0, 2, 4, 8, 12 px, plus pill. Elevation is a two-stop system: a hairline shadow for cards, a deeper one for modals. Avoid soft, diffuse shadows.

04 / Type

Three families, tight roles.

See Brand Guidelines for the full treatment. In product, stick to 500-weight Space Grotesk for UI, JetBrains Mono for meta/telemetry, and reserve Instrument Serif italic for marketing moments only.

05 / Buttons

Four intents, three sizes.

Primary is Steel. Secondary is a neutral card surface. Ghost removes chrome for inline actions. Danger is only for destructive confirmations. Never mix two primaries in one view.

06 / Forms

Inputs, built for density.

All inputs share the same 40px height, the same 4px radius, and focus with a steel-blue ring. Labels are mono-uppercase at 10/.14em to stay distinct from placeholder text.

Max 280 characters. Plain English, not jargon.

07 / Badges, Tags & Status

Small, specific, legible.

Badges carry status semantics — ok, accent, caution, warn. Tags are neutral and dismissible. Never use badges as decoration.

08 / Alerts

Four tones. Use them sparingly.

Info (accent), success (ok), caution, warn. Always lead with a 1-line headline, then a short descriptive sentence. Avoid stacks of alerts — if you have more than two, rethink the flow.

Deployment scheduled for tonight.

The Academy stack will be unavailable between 22:00 and 22:20 ET. No action required.

Form submitted.

We'll reach out within one business day.

Integration disconnected.

Reconnect your workspace to resume syncing volunteers.

09 / Cards

Surfaces for things you scan.

A card is a discrete, tappable unit of content with a title, optional meta, a body, and a mono footer. Hover state elevates subtly — never animates.

10 / Navigation

Two flavors, never both.

Tabs for primary page navigation. Pill-nav for in-component segmentation (filters, view modes). Mixing them on one screen creates uncertainty.
