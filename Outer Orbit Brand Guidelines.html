01 / Logo

The mark is the anchor.

Three orbital bands contained in a circle — a satellite, an atom, a compass. Works embroidered, etched, screen-printed, or in a terminal. Always keeps its geometry.

Clearspace · Minimum size

Clearspace

Minimum X = height of the 'O' in wordmark.

Keep X of clearspace on all sides. Double it on merchandise, embroidery, and wherever the mark is the only graphic on a surface.

Minimum sizes

Digital: 120px wide (full lockup) · 24px (mark)

Print: 0.75" wide (full lockup) · 0.25" (mark). Below these sizes, use the mark alone.

Embroidery + etch

Mark stitches cleanly at 2" or larger. For puffed embroidery, bump line weight by +15%. The mark is approved for single-color metal etch, silicone, debossing.

02 / Color

Mostly ink and vacuum. One signal.

Near-black is the default. Whites are neutral-cool so the system reads like chromed hardware. The accent is steel blue — one industrial signal used like a painted machine surface or a navigation light.

03 / Typography

Geometric sans, with a whisper of serif.

Space Grotesk does the talking. JetBrains Mono adds the engineering texture. Instrument Serif appears in italics as a quiet accent — never as a body face. Most of the brand is set in one of two weights.

05 / Pattern library

Three textures, endlessly remixable.

Grid, orbit, scan. Use patterns as background texture — never as the subject. Layer at 20–40% opacity behind content; full strength only in empty backdrops (decks, covers, hero).

06 / Imagery direction

Hardware-grade product & studio.

Controlled light, charcoal surfaces, honest materials. Photography looks more like a product shoot than a lifestyle reel — even when it's a person. Workshop over studio.

Portrait · B&W · Hard light
Product · Hero · Grey concrete
Surface over subject.

Let the material tell the story — concrete, brushed metal, raw canvas. Subjects live in the scene, never staged against it.

One light source.

Directional, controlled. No flat lighting, no ring lights, no color gels. Shadows are part of the composition.

Never AI art as deliverable.

Use AI freely in process, reference, and mockups. Final brand imagery is photographed or drafted; always captioned when generated.

07 / Voice & tone

Confident. Dry. Slightly understated.

Write like an engineer who used to be in a band. Short sentences. Specific verbs. Low on adjectives. Never cosmic mysticism — Outer Orbit is the vehicle, not the destination.

Principles

Plain English over jargon. If a word sounds like a pitch deck, cut it.

Specifics over superlatives. "Cut onboarding to 4 steps" beats "transformed the experience."

Dry humor is allowed — one joke per page, max.

Lowercase in product UI. Title Case in headlines. UPPERCASE only in mono/meta.

09 / Sub-brands

Each project orbits the same mark.

Sub-brands borrow the system but keep their own typographic identity. The Outer Orbit mark anchors them; a divider + lockup connects parent and child. "Powered by Outer Orbit" is reserved for merch tags and footers.
