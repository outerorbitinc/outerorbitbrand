05 / Voice & Tone

What we say and how.

The Academy speaks like a knowledgeable friend — not a professor, not a startup, not a salesperson. Warm, direct, respectful of your time. We never talk down. We never hype. We just show the path.

Pillar 01

Clear, not clever.

Plain language always wins. If a 60-year-old who's never used AI would have to Google a word, replace it. "Large language model" becomes "AI assistant." "Prompt engineering" becomes "how to ask better questions."

Pillar 02

Encouraging, not cheerful.

There's a difference between honest encouragement and hollow enthusiasm. We don't say "Amazing!" or "You've got this!" We say "That's a real use case — here's how to make it work." Respect over enthusiasm.

Pillar 03

Practical, not theoretical.

Every lesson ends with something you can actually do. Not "now you understand AI" but "here's the exact prompt you can copy and use for your grocery list, your email, your weekend plan." Real life, not demos.

06 / Imagery

Real people, real moments.

Photography should feel like a Sunday morning — warm, unhurried, real. The visual narrative runs from mental chaos (the tornado) through the learning journey to calm confidence. No stock-photo perfection; imperfect, lived-in moments work better for this audience.

Person at kitchen table · laptop · warm morning light · coffee · relaxed expression
Hero imagery — the "before" warmth
50–65 year old, natural setting, slightly overwhelmed but curious. Warm tones. Not staged.
Same person · now smiling · pen & notebook beside laptop · "aha moment" expression
Transformation moment — the "during"
The moment something clicks. Small smile, not ecstasy. Teal tones reinforce the calm of learning.
Person outdoors · perhaps walking · mountains or park · phone in hand · fully at ease
Outcome imagery — the "after"
Peaceful, confident. The mountain walk concept. AI is now invisible — they've just got their life back.
Photography rules
✓ Natural light — no studio flash, no perfect exposure
✓ Real environments — home, café, outdoor. Not conference rooms.
✓ Represent the audience — 50+ faces, diverse backgrounds, reading glasses on the table is fine
✗ No cyborgs, robot arms, glowing AI graphics — this alienates Audience #1
✗ No 30-year-old tech bros. No hoodie-in-front-of-code-screens imagery.
Color treatment on photography
Warm beige-to-teal overlay at 15–20% opacity for hero images. Ties photo to brand palette.
Navy-to-blue overlay for dark section backgrounds. Maintains photo mood while hitting brand colors.
Horizontal fade-to-canvas for side-by-side text + image layouts. Clean, readable.

08 / Brand Bridge

Academy inherits from parent.

How this sub-brand relates to the parent Outer Orbit system — what carries over, what changes, and where the two can coexist.

What Academy inherits
The orbit mark — the core logomark is unchanged. It anchors Academy to the parent brand.
JetBrains Mono — used for metadata, labels, and course codes. Subtle visual thread to the parent.
4px spacing system — same base grid. Layouts compose predictably across both systems.
Steel blue family — the parent's #3B7BC7 and the Academy's blue are in the same hue family. Not identical, but harmonious.
What Academy changes
Background mode — dark (#0B0D10) becomes warm off-white (#F8F7F4). Print-first, accessibility-first.
Type system — Space Grotesk replaced by Plus Jakarta Sans. Warmer, rounder, more approachable to general audiences.
Accent colors — electric neon yellow retired. Teal and sand replace it with human warmth.
Component radius — sharper (--r-2: 4px) becomes softer (--r-2: 6px, --r-pill on buttons). Rounded = approachable.
